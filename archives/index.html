    The Current Team

    Pablo Delclos

    My main interests lie in determining the genetic and environmental mechanisms by which behaviors are developed, with a particular interest in the development of social and mating dynamics both within and among species. To address these behavioral questions, my research applies a multidisciplinary approach, drawing on principles and methodologies ranging from chemical and thermal ecology to genetics, and I ultimately couple these skills to address questions related to how animals develop certain traits and behaviors and why we see animals behave in different ways. To answer these questions at UH-Downtown, I use the house fly system. To learn more specifics, check out our research section. To see more stuff I've done, here's my CV.

     

    Hafsa Khalil

    Hafsa is currently analyzing cuticular hydrocarbon (CHC) data collected from various strains of house fly. She is specifically focused on identifying candidate CHC compounds that are differentially expressed between IIIM and YM male house flies.

     

    Saima Akhtar

    Saima is currently analyzing CHC data collected from various strains of house fly. Her research mostly focuses on identifying sex-specific CHCs whose expression patterns are sensitive to developmental temperature.
